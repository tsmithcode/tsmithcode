# Brand and Engagement Routing

## Purpose

This document keeps TSmithCode.ai and CAD Guardian LLC visibly related while preserving the correct buyer-facing role for each brand.

They operate as one opportunity-routing system, not as interchangeable identities.

## Two distinct lanes

| Lane | Primary role | Typical scope | Canonical destination |
|---|---|---|---|
| **TSmithCode.ai** | Software architecture, modernization, and public technical authority | Legacy .NET modernization, APIs, cloud services, data integration, internal platforms, architecture diagnostics, and software proof kits | [TSmithCode software proof kits](https://tsmithcode.ai/software-proof-kits) |
| **CAD Guardian LLC** | C2C procurement and delivery entity for CAD and engineering automation | Autodesk, AutoCAD, Inventor, Revit, Vault, SolidWorks, MicroStation, PDM/PLM, drawings, BOMs, quoting, and engineering workflow automation | [CAD Guardian](https://www.cadguardian.com/) |

## Shared operating system

Both lanes must feel like they were built by the same organization because they use the same delivery standards:

1. Evidence before claims.
2. A bounded first slice before a large implementation.
3. Public fixtures and synthetic data before private customer artifacts.
4. Deterministic commands, generated receipts, and inspectable decision outputs.
5. GitHub as the source of truth for versioned code and technical evidence.
6. Explicit assumptions, rollback boundaries, acceptance criteria, and risk controls.
7. Confident, concise, enterprise consulting language.

## Routing rule

Keep a software modernization opportunity in the TSmithCode lane when the primary problem is application architecture, .NET, APIs, cloud, data, integration, or internal software delivery.

Route it to CAD Guardian when the primary problem is CAD-native execution, engineering documents, Autodesk or SolidWorks APIs, Vault/PDM/PLM, drawing packages, BOMs, product configuration, or engineering workflow automation.

When an engagement crosses both lanes, use one lead brand and name the second only as the supporting delivery capability. Do not duplicate the same offer under both brands.

## Public-language boundary

- Do not describe TSmithCode.ai as CAD Guardian LLC.
- Do not present CAD Guardian as a general personal resume or broad software portfolio.
- Do not expose private client files, credentials, raw opportunity notes, production data, or private screenshots.
- Do not claim customer results or committed ROI unless the source and publication rights are documented.
- Do not allow unrelated personal brands or experimental identities into buyer-facing proof artifacts.

## Visual family

The brands may share dark navy surfaces, cyan or teal accents, strong white typography, evidence cards, and architecture diagrams.

The language must remain distinct:

- **TSmithCode.ai:** software architecture, modernization, platform boundaries, APIs, reliability, and executive technology decisions.
- **CAD Guardian:** engineering systems, CAD runtimes, drawings, BOMs, PDM/PLM, licensed execution boundaries, and manufacturing or AEC workflows.

## This proof kit

The .NET modernization kit is a TSmithCode.ai artifact. It should route to CAD Guardian only when a future buyer's modernization problem is inseparable from CAD, PDM/PLM, drawings, BOMs, or engineering-system execution.
